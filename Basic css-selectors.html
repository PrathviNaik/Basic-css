Welcome to Microdegree

The term "microdegree" has evolved from its initial use in the 1950s to its current association with shorter, specialized online learning programs, particularly in the tech sector. MicroDegree, founded in 2019, is a company that exemplifies this modern usage, offering vernacular-based IT skills training in India. Here's a more detailed look at the history: Early Usage: The earliest known use of the term "microdegree" dates back to the 1950s, specifically 1957, in the publication Science Progress. The term initially referred to a small unit of angular measurement. Modern Usage: In recent years, "microdegree" has gained traction as a term for shorter, focused online courses, often offered by universities and online learning platforms. These programs are designed to provide specific, job-ready skills in a condensed format, often as an alternative or supplement to traditional degree programs. MicroDegree, for example, provides courses in IT skills like Python, Java, and JavaScript, emphasizing learning in Kannada, a regional language in India. They also offer job assistance upon course completion. MicroDegree's focus on vernacular education and its placement assistance program highlight the modern interpretation of the microdegree concept. Organizations like MIT and DaVinci Coders have also explored the idea of microdegrees in the context of online learning and specialized skills training. The rise of Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) and the increasing demand for specific, job-oriented skills have contributed to the popularity of microdegrees.
