Welcome to Microdegree

The term "microdegree" has evolved from its initial use in the 1950s to its current association with shorter, specialized online learning programs, particularly in the tech sector. MicroDegree, founded in 2019, is a company that exemplifies this modern usage, offering vernacular-based IT skills training in India. Here's a more detailed look at the history: Early Usage: The earliest known use of the term "microdegree" dates back to the 1950s, specifically 1957, in the publication Science Progress. The term initially referred to a small unit of angular measurement. Modern Usage: In recent years, "microdegree" has gained traction as a term for shorter, focused online courses, often offered by universities and online learning platforms. These programs are designed to provide specific, job-ready skills in a condensed format, often as an alternative or supplement to traditional degree programs. MicroDegree, for example, provides courses in IT skills like Python, Java, and JavaScript, emphasizing learning in Kannada, a regional language in India. They also offer job assistance upon course completion. MicroDegree's focus on vernacular education and its placement assistance program highlight the modern interpretation of the microdegree concept. Organizations like MIT and DaVinci Coders have also explored the idea of microdegrees in the context of online learning and specialized skills training. The rise of Massive Open Online Courses (MOOCs) and the increasing demand for specific, job-oriented skills have contributed to the popularity of microdegrees.

WELCOME

Microsoft was founded by Bill Gates and Paul Allen on April 4, 1975, in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Initially, the company focused on developing and selling software, particularly BASIC interpreters for the Altair 8800 computer. Their breakthrough came with the licensing of MS-DOS to IBM for their first personal computer, which helped establish Microsoft as a dominant force in the PC market. Microsoft later introduced Windows, which became the most widely used operating system globally. Today, Microsoft is a multinational technology corporation with a wide range of products and services, including the Windows operating system, the Microsoft Office suite, the Xbox gaming console, and the Azure cloud computing platform. The History Of Microsoft – Feedough Here's a more detailed look at Microsoft's history: Early Years (1975-1980): Microsoft was founded with the goal of creating software for personal computers, starting with BASIC for the Altair 8800. The IBM Partnership (1980s): Microsoft's licensing of MS-DOS to IBM for their personal computer was a pivotal moment, propelling the company to prominence in the PC industry. Windows Revolution (1985 onwards): The introduction of Windows marked a significant shift, bringing a graphical user interface to personal computers and solidifying Microsoft's dominance. Expansion and Diversification: Microsoft expanded its product line to include applications like Word and Excel, entered the gaming market with Xbox, and ventured into cloud computing with Azure. Continued Innovation: Microsoft continues to develop new versions of Windows, innovate in the cloud space, and explore new technologies like artificial intelligence and virtual reality.
