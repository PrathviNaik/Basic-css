WELCOME

Microsoft was founded by Bill Gates and Paul Allen on April 4, 1975, in Albuquerque, New Mexico. Initially, the company focused on developing and selling software, particularly BASIC interpreters for the Altair 8800 computer. Their breakthrough came with the licensing of MS-DOS to IBM for their first personal computer, which helped establish Microsoft as a dominant force in the PC market. Microsoft later introduced Windows, which became the most widely used operating system globally. Today, Microsoft is a multinational technology corporation with a wide range of products and services, including the Windows operating system, the Microsoft Office suite, the Xbox gaming console, and the Azure cloud computing platform. The IBM Partnership (1980s): Microsoft's licensing of MS-DOS to IBM for their personal computer was a pivotal moment, propelling the company to prominence in the PC industry. Windows Revolution (1985 onwards): The introduction of Windows marked a significant shift, bringing a graphical user interface to personal computers and solidifying Microsoft's dominance. Expansion and Diversification: Microsoft expanded its product line to include applications like Word and Excel, entered the gaming market with Xbox, and ventured into cloud computing with Azure. Continued Innovation: Microsoft continues to develop new versions of Windows, innovate in the cloud space, and explore new technologies like artificial intelligence and virtual reality.
